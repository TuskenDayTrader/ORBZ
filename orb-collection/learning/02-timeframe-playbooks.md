# 02) Timeframe Playbooks (5m, 15m, 30m, 60m)

## What this lesson is for

This lesson explains how to use each chart in a simple way.

The goal is to keep trading easy:

- **60 minute chart** = trend
- **30 minute chart** = day structure
- **15 minute chart** = setup quality
- **5 minute chart** = entry

Do not use every chart for the same job.
Each chart has one main job.

---

## 1) The 60 minute chart

### Main job
The 60 minute chart tells you the **big direction**.

Use it to answer:

- Is the market trending up?
- Is the market trending down?
- Is the market sideways?
- Is the trend strong or weak?

### What to look for

For an uptrend:
- higher highs
- higher lows
- price above the 89 EMA
- 5 EMA above 13 EMA above 89 EMA

For a downtrend:
- lower highs
- lower lows
- price below the 89 EMA
- 5 EMA below 13 EMA below 89 EMA

### What to do with it

The 60 minute chart tells you:

- whether to prefer longs or shorts
- whether the day has trend potential
- whether ORB breakout trades are likely to work

### Simple rule
If the 60 minute chart is not clear, trade smaller or wait.

---

## 2) The 30 minute chart

### Main job
The 30 minute chart shows the **day structure**.

Use it to find:

- major support and resistance
- overnight highs and lows
- previous day levels
- whether the market is building balance or expansion

### What to look for

- Are candles overlapping?
- Is price moving in one direction?
- Is the market compressing before a break?
- Did a key level already break and hold?

### What to do with it

The 30 minute chart helps you avoid bad trades.
It tells you if the market has room to run.

### Simple rule
If the 30 minute chart is trapped in a tight range, expect more fakeouts.

---

## 3) The 15 minute chart

### Main job
The 15 minute chart shows **setup quality**.

Use it to decide if a trade is worth taking.

### What to look for

For longs:
- higher highs and higher lows
- support holding
- EMA 5 curling above EMA 13
- price staying above the 89 EMA

For shorts:
- lower highs and lower lows
- resistance holding
- EMA 5 curling below EMA 13
- price staying below the 89 EMA

### What to do with it

The 15 minute chart helps you:

- place stops more carefully
- set realistic targets
- avoid chasing the 5 minute chart too early

### Simple rule
If the 15 minute chart disagrees with your trade, do not force the trade.

---

## 4) The 5 minute chart

### Main job
The 5 minute chart is the **entry chart**.

Use it to find:

- the opening range
- the EMA pullback
- the breakout and retest
- the exact place to enter

### What to look for

For longs:
- price above the 89 EMA
- 5 EMA above 13 EMA
- price pulling back into the 5 EMA or 13 EMA
- breakout above ORB or key level
- candle closes strong

For shorts:
- price below the 89 EMA
- 5 EMA below 13 EMA
- price pulling back into the 5 EMA or 13 EMA
- breakout below ORB or key level
- candle closes weak

### What to do with it

Use the 5 minute chart only after the higher timeframes already make sense.

### Simple rule
Do not let the 5 minute chart trick you into trading against the 60 minute trend.

---

## 5) How the charts work together

### Long trade sequence
1. 60 minute chart says trend is up
2. 30 minute chart shows the market has room to rise
3. 15 minute chart shows support holding
4. 5 minute chart gives the entry

### Short trade sequence
1. 60 minute chart says trend is down
2. 30 minute chart shows the market has room to fall
3. 15 minute chart shows resistance holding
4. 5 minute chart gives the entry

### No-trade sequence
If the charts disagree, stay out.

---

## 6) ORB playbook by timeframe

### ORB-5
Use ORB-5 when:
- the open is clean
- the trend is already strong
- volume is active
- you want an early entry

### ORB-15
Use ORB-15 when:
- the first 5 minutes are noisy
- you want more confirmation
- the market is less obvious

### ORB-30
Use ORB-30 when:
- you want a better structure read
- you want fewer fakeouts
- you are trading a balanced open

### IB-60
Use IB-60 when:
- you want to know the full opening structure
- the market may trend after 10:30 ET
- you want the day’s bigger framework

---

## 7) EMA playbook by timeframe

### 60 minute chart
Use EMAs to define the trend.

- 5 above 13 above 89 = bullish strength
- 5 below 13 below 89 = bearish strength

### 15 minute chart
Use EMAs to judge setup quality.

- Is price holding above support?
- Is price rejecting resistance?
- Is the trend still healthy?

### 5 minute chart
Use EMAs to trigger the entry.

- wait for pullback
- wait for 5 EMA and 13 EMA to realign
- enter after the candle confirms

---

## 8) Simple trade examples

### Example 1: Trend long
- 60 minute chart is up
- 30 minute chart shows no major resistance overhead
- 15 minute chart pulls back and holds
- 5 minute chart breaks ORB and retests
- Enter long
- Stop below the pullback low
- Target at least 2.1R

### Example 2: Trend short
- 60 minute chart is down
- 30 minute chart shows space below
- 15 minute chart rejects resistance
- 5 minute chart breaks ORB low and holds
- Enter short
- Stop above the pullback high
- Target at least 2.1R

### Example 3: No trade
- 60 minute chart is flat
- 15 minute chart is messy
- 5 minute chart gives many false signals
- Stay out

---

## 9) Beginner mistakes

### Mistake 1: Trading every chart
Do not do this.
Each chart has a job.

### Mistake 2: Trading against the 60 minute trend
This usually lowers your odds.

### Mistake 3: Chasing the first breakout candle
Wait for confirmation.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring the 15 minute chart
The 15 minute chart tells you whether the move has room.

### Mistake 5: Forcing EMA trades in chop
If EMAs are tangled, the market is not clean.

---

## 10) Simple drill

Before every trade, ask these 4 questions:

1. What is the 60 minute trend?
2. What does the 30 minute chart say about structure?
3. Does the 15 minute chart support the idea?
4. Does the 5 minute chart give a clean entry?

If all 4 answers agree, the trade is worth studying.
If not, wait.

---

## 11) Summary

- **60 minute chart** = trend
- **30 minute chart** = structure
- **15 minute chart** = setup quality
- **5 minute chart** = entry

Use the charts in order.
Do not let the smaller chart override the bigger chart unless the bigger chart also changes.

The best trades are simple:

- clear trend
- clear structure
- clear entry
- clear stop
- clear target
