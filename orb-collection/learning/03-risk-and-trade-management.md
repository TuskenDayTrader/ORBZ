# 03) Risk and Trade Management

## Stop loss (simple guidance)

Your stop loss is the price that proves your trade idea is wrong.

### Long example

- OR high = 42,100
- Long entry = 42,108
- Stop below breakout/retest structure = 42,094
- Risk = 14 points

### Short example

- OR low = 41,980
- Short entry = 41,972
- Stop above breakout/retest structure = 41,986
- Risk = 14 points

## Take profit and R:R examples

Use planned targets before entry.

| Target type | Formula (long example) | Result if risk = 14 points |
|---|---|---|
| 1R | Entry + 1 × risk | 42,122 |
| 2R | Entry + 2 × risk | 42,136 |
| 3R | Entry + 3 × risk | 42,150 |

Tip: many beginners can scale out at 1R and hold a smaller runner for 2R+.

## Position sizing basics

Simple rule:

1. Pick max dollar risk per trade (example: $50).
2. Find stop distance in points.
3. Convert points to dollar risk per contract.
4. Contracts = max dollar risk ÷ risk per contract.

If result is not a whole number, round down.

## Volume confirmation

Use volume to avoid weak breakouts.

- Better signal: breakout bar volume is above recent average.
- Weaker signal: breakout happens on low volume.
- If volume is weak, either skip the trade or reduce size.

## False breakout guidance

Watch for these warning signs:

- Breakout candle closes outside range, then next candle quickly returns inside.
- Breakout has low volume.
- Breakout fails at a known resistance/support area.

Simple response:

- Exit quickly if your invalidation is hit.
- Do not widen stop loss.
- Wait for a new valid setup.
